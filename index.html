PEACHY PRINTER BUILD INSTRUCTIONS
BETA VERSION - PP28

Section 1 - Preparing for the assembly process

In this section you will be preparing your workspace, gathering the necessary tools, and preparing the printer components for assembly.

1. Find a large, clean, well lit surface to work on.
2. Make sure you have access to the following tools:
- Utility knife
- Small file with pointed corner
- Ruler (metric or imperial)
- Cyanoacrylate™ glue (CA glue, AKA superglue)
- Needle nose pliers
- permanent marker or pencil

Section 2 - Testing the snap-fit mirror frame

In this section you will be testing the snap-fitting action of the mirror frame and other components. It is important to gain an experience with how the printer snaps together before you actually begin the assembly process.

For this section you will need:

- One of the Mirror Frames
- Static damper mount B (Mirror frame mount B)
- Spring force magnet holder (static magnet holder)

1. Slide the Mirror Frame into the large side of the slot on the Spring Force Magnet Holder (Static Magnet Holder) so that the 1 arrows (1 →) are the same side and point at each other, then snap one side of the mirror frame into the narrow end of the slot, then snap the other side.
2. Clip Static Damper Mount B (Mirror frame mount B) to Mirror Frame B so that the 2 arrows (2 →) are on the same side and point at each other when snapped together.
3. Disassemble the parts you just assembled.

Section 3 - Assembling the Thread Assembly

For this section you will need:
- Elastic thread
- Carbon Rod
- 2 Acetate Arrows (there is no difference between the two)
- The magnets (For this step we will be using the smaller magnets, but we find it is best to keep them stuck to the large magnets so they do not get lost.)
- 2 Mirrors (Leave the protective film on the mirrors)
- Metal tweezers
- CA Glue

1. Take the acetate arrow and fold the magnet tab to a 90 degree angle, either way.
2. Take 2 of the smaller magnets, and stack them, they will naturally want to do this.
3. Pinch the smaller magnets in between the tweezers tips. While doing so, use your fingernail to split the smaller magnets, making sure they are still aligned in a way they are attracted to each other, so that one magnet is on each tip of the tweezers.
4. Pinch the magnet tab between the magnets with the tweezers, slide the tweezers away while using your thumb and forefinger to hold the magnets in place. The magnets should remain on each side of the magnet tab, near the middle.
5. Put a small dab of CA glue onto the magnets so they are glued to the acetate arrow.
6. With the elastic thread, tie a knot (near the one end of the elastic thread) loosely so that there is a large loop, insert the carbon rod through the loop and then tighten the knot onto the rod.
7. Cut the short end of excess thread off about 1mm away from the carbon rod.
8. Cut the carbon rod to a length of about 4 mm, the knot should be in the middle of the piece you cut.
9. Cut the long end of the excess elastic thread to about 15 cm long (approximate).
10. Repeat steps 6 - 8 so you have 2 of these.
11. Put the elastic thread through the hole on one end of the acetate arrow so that the carbon rod is on the opposite face as the magnets.
12. Take the second piece of elastic thread and put it through the hole on the other end of the arrow, so the carbon rod is on the opposite face as the magnets. We will now call this a thread assembly.
13. Repeat steps 1-12 so that you have two thread assemblies.

Section 4 - Mounting the Thread Assemblies to the Mirror Frames

For this section you will need:
- Both Thread Assemblies
- Both Mirror Frames
- Both mirrors
- Cyanoacrylate Glue
- Utility Knife

1. Lay the mirror assembly frame down so that the arrows are facing up. Then take and lay the thread assembly across the top of the frame with the side without the magnets butted up against the inside edge of the mirror assembly frame on the side with the opening.
2. Take the thread extending off the side of the thread assembly without the mirrors and lace it through the center notch on the edge of the mirror assembly frame, then lace the thread around the upper and lower notches multiple times to lock it in place. Make sure the acetate arrow is butted up against the mirror frame. Apply glue to the elastic thread where it wraps around the upper notch and then cut off the excess thread.
3. Pinch the thread on the magnet side of the Thread Assembly and pull it across and into the center notch on the other edge of the Mirror Assembly frame. Ensure that the magnets are centered at the dashes in between the two mounting points on the outer edges of the mirror assembly frame.
4. Repeat the same process of wrapping the thread around the mirror assembly as shown in step 2. Cut off any excess thread. Once finished the thread assembly should rotate freely. Your mirror assembly should look like the last image ine the set.
5. Fold the dynamic damper plate out 90 degrees so that it protrudes to the side opposite of the magnets.
6. Remove the film from the back of the mirror and place the mirror onto its mounting spot on the acetate arrow so it is on the same side as the magnets. We will now refer to this as the Mirror Assembly.

Section 5 - Adding the Spring Force Magnets and Coils to the Mirror Assembly

For this section you will need:
- Clear tubing
- Large square magnets
- Coils
- Both Mirror assemblies
- Utility knife
- Needle nose pliers
- spring force mounting plate
- permanent marker or pencil

1. Using needle nose pliers, insert them into the clear tubing while closed and then open them to stretch the opening of the tube.
2. Insert the square magnets into the stretched end of the clear tubing so that the north and south poles are sideways, touching the walls of the tubing. If you inserted the magnets in the correct orientation, they should attract to each other through the walls of the tubing.
3. Cut the clear tubing 3 cm (about 1") back from the stretched end.
4. Repeat steps 2 - 4 until you have four 3 cm tubes with magnets inside them. *If your magnets do not attract like this, they are not positioned correctly*. We will now call these the spring force adjustment tubes.
5. Clip the mirror frame into the Spring Force Mounting Plate as you did in Step 1 of Section 1.
6. Insert the non-magnet end of a spring force adjustment tube into the hole that extends off of the slot on the spring force mounting plate. The excess tube must extend off of the mounting plate in the opposite direction of the mirror assembly
7. Rotate the spring force adjustment tube until the face of the acetate arrow is level with the face of the mirror assembly frame. The dynamic damper arm must be extending away from the spring force adjustment tube.
8. Using a marker or pencil, mark on the spring force adjustment tube the side that is closest to the thread assembly. Remove the spring force adjustment tube from the hole and glue the coil to the marked side centered directly on the magnet. Make sure that the coil is positioned so that the leads extend in the same direction as the excess tube coming off the magnet.
9. Insert the spring force adjustment tube and coil back into the hole on the spring force adjustment plate. Your thing should look like this.
10. Insert another spring force adjustment tube into the second hole on the mounting plate. If necessary, rotate the adjustment tube so the face of the acetate arrow is parallel with the face of the mirror assembly frame.
11. Repeat steps 5-10 on the other mirror assembly.

Section 6 - Mounting the Mirror Assemblies to the L Bracket

For this section you will need:
- Both Mirror Assemblies
- The L Bracket
- Male and Female Static Damper Connectors (both sets)
- Static damper mount B (Mirror frame mount B)
- Cyanoacrylate glue
- Neon Tubing

1. Cut two 1 cm (3/8") pieces from the neon tubing and split one wall of each tube all the way down the side in the center.
2. On the Static Damper Mount and the L Bracket, mount the neon tubes around the inside edges a of the narrow half of the Damper Slider Channel (the slot).
3. Apply glue to both sides of the tubes where it meets the plastic.
4. Clip Static Damper Mount B (Mirror frame mount B) to Mirror Frame B so that the 2 arrows (2 →) are on the same side and point at each other when snapped together.
5. Clip Mirror Frame A onto the center of the L bracket so that the 2 arrows (2 →) point at each other.
6. Clip Static Damper Mount B into the L bracket so that the 3 arrows (3 →) point at each other when snapped into place.

Section 7 - Assembling and Oiling the Static Dampers

For this section you will need:
- Male Static Damper Connector
- Female Damper Connector
- The L bracket with both mirror assemblies mounted.
- Oil dripper

1. Take a Male Static Damper Connector and insert it into the slider channel in the L bracket so that the male end points toward the Spring force magnet holder (static magnet holder)
2. Clip the Female Damper Connector onto the Male Damper Connector so that the acetate arrow is inside the arch on the Female Damper Connector.
3. Take a Male Static Damper Connector and insert it into the slider channel in the Static damper mount B (Mirror frame mount B) so that the male end points toward the Spring force magnet holder (static magnet holder)
4. Clip the Female Damper Connector onto the Male Damper Connector so that the acetate arrow is inside the arch on the Female Damper Connector.
5. Make sure the Dynamic Damper Tab is lifted on the acetate arrow and pointing up at 90 degrees.
6. Adjust the Female Damper Connector so that it is almost touching the Dynamic damper tab on the acetate arrow (less than 1mm away, but not touching it).
7. Using a fine tip applicator apply a very small dab of oil in between each of the Female Static Damper Connectors and the Dynamic Dampers, making sure not to drip oil elsewhere on your printer.

Section 8 - Making the laser focus adjustable

For this section you will need:
- The Laser
- Needle nose pliers
- The Laser Aperture Disc

1. Take your laser and remove the focus ring from the laser.
2. Take the needle nose pliers and remove the threaded lens housing (the black threads) from the laser.
3. Place the laser aperture disc on top of the lens in the threaded lens housing. Screw the focus ring back on, sandwiching the aperture disc in place.
4. Holding the black thread with the pair of needle nose pliers, tighten the focus ring onto the threaded lens housing tightly.
5. Screw the threaded lens housing and focus ring back into the laser body.

Section 9 - Mounting the Laser to the L bracket For this section you will need:

For this section you will need:
- The Laser
- Laser Bracket A and B
- L Bracket

1. Take a piece of neon tubing and cut it so it is 4cm.
2. Stand the neon tubing on its end and use a sharp knife to split one wall of the neon tubing down the center.
3. Take the neon tubing and slide it onto the inside edge of the ring on Laser Bracket A so that the slit on the tube hugs both sides of the plastic.
4. Slide Laser Bracket A onto the L Bracket so that the arrows ( → ) in arrow set number 4 point at each other.
5. Slide Laser Bracket B onto the L Bracket. so that the arrows ( → ) in arrow set number 4B point at each other.
6. Insert the laser through the hole in Laser Bracket B, so the Focus Ring is being held by the Neon Tubing on Laser Bracket A.

Section 10 - Mounting the Circuit

For this section you will need:
- Circuit
- Circuit mounting plate
- The L Bracket with Mirror Assemblies and Laser attached
- The Stand off
- 4 small bolts
- 4 small nuts
- small phillips head screwdriver

1. Hold the Circuit Mounting Plate so that the etchings are face down and and the arrow is pointing to the right. Then put the circuit down on top of the mounting plate so that the audio jack is positioned overtop of the arrow.
2. Insert 4 small bolts into each hole on the Circuit so they go through the Circuit Mount.
3. Thread the nuts onto the bolts so the Circuit is held on tightly. Note: The head of the bolts should be on the Circuit's) (side), the nuts on the Circuit's) Mount (side).
4. Insert the circuit mounting plate onto the slot on the L Bracket so that arrow 5 points directly at the 5 on the snapping nub of the circuit mounting plate.
5. Insert the Stand off arm into the L bracket so that arrow 6 points directly at the 6 on the snapping nub of the Stand off arm.

Section 11 - Hooking the Coils up to the Circuit

For this section you will need:
- Coils
- Stripped wire
- Sand paper or lighter

1-A. Take the coil leads and sand the coating off 1” back from the end of both wires. Sand gently about 25 strokes covering all "sides" of the wire while being careful not to break the wire.
1-B. Take the coils leads and heat the end (1 ") of both wires with a lighter so that the coating is removed. Be careful not to hold the flame too close, as it will melt the wire.
2. Take one lead wire off each coil and twist an inch of each of them around each other.
3. Take the 2 coil wires you just twisted together, and wrap them around the stripped wire.
4. Insert the stripped wire with the connected coil leads wrapped around it into Port 2 on the circuit, so that the coil wires are inserted into the port.
5. Take the other lead wire of one of the coils and wrap it around another stripped wire.
6. Repeat step 4 with the second coil on its own stripped wire.
7. Take one of the stripped wires with a single coil lead wire wrapped around it and insert it into port 1, so the coil wire itself is inserted inside the port.
8. Take the other stripped with with a coil lead wire wrapped around it and insert it into Port 3 so the coil wire itself is inserted inside the port.

Section 12 - Assembling the safety switch

For this section you will need:
- The Main Hanger
- 4 Small Bolts
- 4 Small Nuts
- Wire
- Aluminum Foil
- Acetate Pants
- Clear Tape (Scotch tape)

1. First we will make the Safety Switch Plate. Take the Acetate Pants and gently fold them making a shallow crease that continues the pre cut line at the end of the plastic. The crease should only go halfway up the pants, not all the way to the other end.
2. Push each leg of the acetate pants together slightly so their is a slight overlap, and tape it. It should make a shallow valley between the legs of the pants.
3. Tape a piece of Aluminum Foil to the inside of the valley covering the slit in the pants that you just taped. Leaving at least 3 inches (6.5cm) of extra Aluminum Foil extending off the edge without the holes in it.
4. Fold the extra Aluminum Foil around the underside of the valley and to the end of the pants so that it is covering the 2 holes.
5. Carefully poke a hole in the Aluminum Foil where the holes in the acetate pants are. We will now call this the safety switch plate.
6. Insert a small bolt in each hole in the safety switch plate so the head is sitting on the valley side of the Aluminum Foil. Cut away any extra Aluminum Foil. You can now set the safety switch plate aside.
7. Take the piece of wire and cut it so you have 2 wires that are each 18 cm (7 inches) long.
8. Strip 8 cm off of one end of one wire, and 1 cm (3/8 inch) off the other end of the same wire.
9. Strip 2 cm (¾ inch) off of one end of the other wire, and 1 cm (3/8 inch) off the other end of the same wire.
10. Take the Main Hanger and insert one bolt into each of the holes on the side of the safety switch plate. Ensure that the bolt heads are sitting on the face opposite to the etchings.
11. Take the wire with the longest stripped end. Wrap the long striped piece of wire around the one of the bolts on the Main Hanger, then run it across the channel and wrap it around the bolt on the other side. Screw a new onto each bolt, clamping the wire in place. Make sure the wire is mounted to the same side of the Main Hanger as the labelling.
12. Mount the safety switch plate to the main hanger by sticking the two bolts into the two closely spaced holes in the main hanger. Note - You should insert the bolts into the main hanger from the side opposite of the etchings. Thread a nut onto one of the bolts tightening the safety switch in place.
13. Take the second wire (the 18cm wire with 1 cm of wire cut off one end and 2cm off the other) and wrap the longer stripped end around the other bolt on the safety switch plate, then screw a nut onto the bolt, pinching the wire into place. You should now have one remaining stripped end on each safety switch wire.
14. Strip about 5mm of the ends of both laser wires.
15. Take the end of a safety switch wire and wrap it around the bare end of one of the wires coming from the laser. Repeat this with the other safety switch wire, connecting it to the other laser wire. It does not matter which safety switch wire gets connected to which laser wire.
16. Insert the ground (black) wire end (with the other wire wrapped around it) into Port 5 on the circuit.

Section 13 - Mounting the Laser Assembly to the Main Hanger

For this section you will need:
- The Main hanger
-The Laser assembly (The L bracket with the Circuit, Laser, and Mirror assemblies mounted.)
- Stand Off

1. Connect the Laser Assembly to the Main Hanger by inserting the Stand off arm and Circuit Mounting Plate into mounting slots 7 and 8 respectively. The 7 on the Circuit Mounting Plate should be facing the 7 on the Main Hanger, and the 8 on the Stand off arm should be facing the 8 on the Main Hanger.
2. Remove the protective film from the reflective side of the mirror. Be very careful not to touch the mirror with your fingers.

Section 14 - Assembling the Internal Box

For this section you will need:
- The Internal Box
- The Main Hanger with Laser Assembly attached

1. Take the internal box and fold its side walls so that they extend out at a 90 degree angle from the back wall. Set it down so that it is standing on its edge and the 3 mounting tabs are pointing up.
2. Take the main hanger (with L bracket and mirror assemblies attached) and mount it on top of the internal box so that the three tabs go through the three slots on Main Hanger. Be sure that the Laser Assembly is hanging down into the Internal Box.

Section 15 - Assembling the External Housing

For this section you will need:
- The External Housing Cardboard Piece
- Elastic Thread
- The Steel Ball
- The large Orange Sheet
- Clear Tape (Scotch Tape)
- Cyanoacrylate glue (CA glue AKA superglue)

1. Use CA glue to glue the steel ball to the end of 3 inches of elastic thread.
2. Put the elastic thread through the hole in the Orange Sheet so that the Steel Ball is on the inside of the existing fold, and tape the elastic thread so that the ball is hanging about 1 inch from the orange sheet. Cut off the excess elastic thread.
3. Fold the Orange Sheet along the dotted lines on the outer edges, so that the folds go into the inside of the inside fold.
4. Take the outer housing and fold the two sidewalls so that they sit approximately 90 degrees from the back wall. Place the outer housing down on the edges with the notches cut in it.
5. Take the orange plastic sheet and tape it onto the outer housing so that it closes the top and front faces of the box. Your steel ball should be hanging from the back right corner of the box. the Steel Ball should be hanging on the right side.
6. Slide the Outer Box over the Inner Box.
7. Now test the safety switch, first make sure the safety switch is not touching the wire beneath when the steel ball is not on top of it.
8. Tip and shake the external housing, the steel ball should fall off the switch long before the printer tips to an angle where the laser could shine out of the housing.
9. Place the Upper cardboard plate on top the outer housing.

Section 16 - Assembling the Drip system

For this section you will need:
- Clear Tubing
- Neon Tubing
- Syringe
- 3 Clamp Rings
- 3 Larger Bolts (they fit into the clamp rings)
- 2 Nuts
- Top Piece of Cardboard
- Hose Hanger
- Needle nose pliers with wire cutter
- Wire stripper
- Hollow Aluminum Rod
- Audio Cable
- Upper and Lower liquid reservoirs

1. Take the Hollow Aluminum Rod and cut it in half using the wire cutter on the needle nose pliers. Use the end of the needle nose pliers to pinch one end shut. Then make sure the other three remain open.
2. Take the rod with the closed end and file the points on both creased edges of the closed end. Once finished, the closed end should be no wider than the rest of the rod.
3. Take the rod with the closed end and file nearly halfway through the aluminum rod creating a small hole positioned in the middle of the rod. Be careful not to bend the rod.
4. Disassemble the syringe. take the black plunger gasket and poke a hole (smaller than the aluminum rod) in the center of the peak.
5. Cut four 1 cm pieces of neon tubing. Take one and insert it into the plunger, put the others aside for now.
6. Take the Rod with 2 open ends and push it through the Neon Tubing and the Plunger so the Plunger is near the middle of the Rod.
7. Insert the Rod and Plunger into the large opening of the syringe.
8. Take the Rod with a closed end and push the closed end into the small hole. (where a needle normally goes) You might need to file the edges of the aluminum rod more to get it to fit into the syringe, but make sure it is still closed. The hole you filed in the side must be inside the syringe. Water needs to flow through this hole from within the syringe so it must not be blocked.
9. Take a second piece of 1 cm neon tubing and slide it onto the excess aluminum tube that is extending from the bottom hole in the syringe. Push it all the way up so that it climbs over and covers the end of the syringe.
10. Take the Audio Jack and cut off one end, then strip about 4 inches off the end. Cut off the insulating thread.
11. Burn off the protective coating on the ends of all three wires, being careful not to melt the wires.
12. Twist the red and blue wires together.
13. Take the red and blue wires and wrap it around one of the larger bolts, right near the head. Thread a nut all the way onto the bolt till it snugly clamps the wire in place.
14. Repeat step 13 with the remaining wire from the audio cable and another bolt/nut.
15. Take two clamp rings and thread one onto each of the bolts just a couple of turns.
16. Take your Syringe and push the rod with the rubber plunger in until it just barely touches the other aluminum rod.
17. Take both of the Clamp Rings (with wires) and clamp one on each end of the aluminum rods extending out of the top and bottom ends of the syringe. It does not matter which clamp goes on which end. Be very careful not to crush the aluminum rod by over tightening the clamp.
18. Take another 1cm piece neon tubing and put it on the rod coming out of the bottom of the syringe, so it buts up against the Clamp Ring.
19. Cut a two 10 cm pieces of neon tubing. Take one of them and make an angle cut on the end of the hose so that a Clamp ring can be slid over it. Use the pliers to pull the neon tubing through the clamp ring 1 cm beyond the angle cut. Then trim off the angle piece leaving at least 1 cm of neon tubing.
20. Apply glue all the way around the outer edge of the Hose Hanger and glue it to the middle of the neon tubing with the clamp on it. It’s important that the hose is glued well enough that it follows the curve of the Hose Hanger.
21. Move the clamp so that it is at the end of the Hose Hanger.
22. Repeat step 20 with the remaining 10cm piece of tubing so that you have another hose hanger (this one doesn't need a clamp).
23. Slide the end of the neon tube with the clamp on it onto the aluminum rod sticking out of the top of the syringe. Be sure their is space between the rod in the tube, and where the O clamp on the neon tube starts, so the O clamp does not pinch the rod inside the tube.
24. Take the outer housing off of your printer and slide your lower reservoir into the internal housing so that it sits directly below the laser assembly.
25. You must now cut two sections of clear hose to make each end of your drip system reach into the upper and lower reservoirs. If you are sticking with our default printer setup, then follow step 26-A. If you are making your own custom reservoir setup, Follow step 26-B.
26-A. Take the clear tubing and cut it to a length of 18 inches. Use your needle nose pliers to stretch both ends so it's easier to slide onto the neon tubing. Slide the clear hose over the neon tubing on the bottom of the syringe, and then take your 2nd hose hanger and slide one end of the neon tubing into the bottom of the clear tubing. You can now place the Bottom end of the drip feed inside your lower reservoir.
27-A. Put your outer housing back on (the drip feed hose should exit through the side of the outer housing where the notch is cut). Put the upper cardboard plate on the External housing, then place your upper reservoir on top. Place the hose hanger on the edge of your upper reservoir so that the drip feed is hanging down the outside of it.
28-A. Measure the distance between the end of the neon tube on the hose hanger to the bottom of your upper reservoir. Cut a piece of clear tubing to that length, stretch one end of it with your pliers and then slide it onto the neon tube on the end of the hose hanger (you may want to remove the hose hanger from the upper reservoir to connect the clear tubing.
29-A. You may now hang your drip feed from the upper reservoir (the clear tubing you just connected should reach the bottom of the upper reservoir).
26-B. The length of these clear tubing sections will depend on the size of your upper and lower reservoirs, as well as the total distance between the two reservoirs. Cut them to the appropriate length and then slide the two pieces of clear tubing over the neon tubing at the top and bottom of your drip feed.
27-B. You can now hang the top end of your drip feed in the upper reservoir, letting the drip feed run down into the lower reservoir. We recommend securing your drip feed to something solid on at least one point.

Congratulations, you've successfully assembled the Peachy Printer! You're almost ready to start printing…

Section 18 - Preparing for Calibration

For this section you will need:
- The two USB cables
- The Audio cable
- The Peachy Printer
- Your computer

1. Install the Peachy Printer Software.
2. Remove the outer housing and connect the 2 USB ports and the Audio jack to the ports on the circuit of the printer. Run them down and out the side. Place the calibration grid inside the Internal Housing directly beneath the Main Hanger. When you place the external housing back on, your cables should exit through the small notch cut at the bottom of the external housing.
3. Plug the USB and audio cables into your computer. Note - make sure you plug the audio cable connected to the drip feed into the microphone jack in your computer, and the audio cable from the circuit into the headphone jack in your computer.
