Section 1 - Assembling The Mirror Assemblies

For this section you will need:

- __

1. Wrap the elastic thread around corner thread notch set A, and channel it through center notch A.

2. To prepare the dynamic dampers to be slid onto the elastic thread, take two dynamic dampers and slide the carbon rod all the way through the hole in the center of each one.

3. Take the 2 dynamic dampers and put the thread through the holes as you would thread a needle.

4. Making sure the elastic thread is hooked into the center thread notch. Take the elastic thread and pull it across the tool-frame and onto the opposite center thread notch so it sits loosely.

5. To set the proper tension, pinch the elastic thread at spot (To be named) and pull the point at which you pinched to center notch B.  Then wrap the elastic thread around the corner notches many times to lock it in place. Tape the elastic thread in place at the corner.

6. Take 2 carbon rods (pencil leads) and place one over each + and - spot on the right and left sides of the tool-frame. Cut the rods at each + and - spot using a razor blade. Your carbon rod should now be the exact same length as the distance between spot + and - on their respective sides of the tool-frame. IMPORTANT - The + and - marks on side A and B of the tool-frame are different lengths. You must cut one carbon rod on side A and one carbon rod on side B, and those carbon rods must stay on their respective sides.

7. Slide the dynamic dampers to the (appropriate) marks on the tool-frame.

8. Sharpen one end of each carbon rod by rubbing them at an angle against a piece of (XXX grit) sandpaper.

9. Making sure not to damage the dynamic damper, push the sharp end of the shorter carbon rod through the hole in the dynamic damper on the Side A of the tool-frame. Be careful not to fold it bend, and make sure the dynamic damper stays at the (appropriate) mark.

10. Repeat step 9 with the longer carbon rod on Side B of the tool-frame.

11. Slide the thread hooks onto the tool-frame on the thread hook marks. Ensure that the thin wall of each thread hook is facing up.

12. Glue the elastic thread to the thread hooks, you must only put glue on the thread hook, do not let any glue get on the thread past the end of the thread hook. If you do get any glue on the thread past the thread hook the elastic thread will lose some elasticity and your printer may not work properly.

13. With the carbon rod between the C and E marks, glue 5mm of each end of the carbon rod to the elastic thread. Again make sure not to allow any glue to get on the elastic thread outside the edges.

14. Take 2 (two) of the smaller magnets and stack them, they will naturally want to do this. Pinch the smaller magnets in between the tweezers tips. While doing so, use your fingernail to split the smaller magnets, making sure they are still aligned in a way they are attracted to each other.

15. Put a small dab of glue on two smaller magnets, and carefully glue them onto the elastic thread at point +, against the end of the carbon rod. Touch the elastic thread with one magnet first to make sure it is in place, then crimp the thread between the magnets. If there is a small space between the magnets and the end of the carbon rod, apply glue over it to bridge the gap. Never pinch the carbon rod between the magnets, because if your magnets get glued there your printer will not work properly.

16. Repeat step 15 on the other end.

17. Take the long mirror and put a line of glue on the backside of the long mirror.

18. Making sure the surface of the long mirror is parallel to the long end of the magnets, glue the long mirror to the end of the longer carbon rod at the marked spot.

19. Repeat steps 17-18 with the small mirror on the shorter carbon rod.

21. Cut the elastic thread at the outer edge of each thread hook.

22. Remove the thread hooks from the tool-frame, you will now have 2 of what we will call the thread assembly.

23. Take the shorter thread assembly and put the thread hooks onto the outside edge of the shorter frame, so that the elastic thread is centered within the frame. Do the same with the longer thread assembly and frame.

24. Take 2 coils and cut the excess paper off one side of each coil while being extremely careful not to cut the wire of the coil.

25. Take the coils and sand the end of the wires, and continue to sand down about 1 inch length of the coil wire. Sand gently about 25 strokes on all “sides” of the wire.

26. Take a dab of glue and glue one coil to mirror assembly frame A where shown, making sure the smaller magnets do not touch the coil when it turns.

27. Grab your spring force adjustment plates and glue a square of acetate plastic to each one as shown.

28. Clip the spring force adjustment plate onto mirror assembly frame A, overtop of the hash marks near the end of the frame, above the edge of the coil on the bottom side of the mirror assembly frame.

29. Take the static damper mounting plate, and glue it to the mirror assembly frame on the notches just off from center.

30. Cut 4 (four) 1 cm pieces of neon tubing, sit them down so they are standing on their ends, and split one wall of each piece of the neon tubing.

31. Put a piece of neon tubing on each side of the static damper, aligned with the bottom of the hole near the flat end as shown.

32. Lay the static damper down, and cut a V shape into the two neon tubes from the rounded end of the static damper.

33. Flip the static damper over, and apply a line of glue along the peak of each hose on the backside of the static damper, gluing it to the static damper mounting plate.

34. Take one lead wire of the coil and wrap it around one of the screw terminal posts

35. Take the other lead wire of the coil and wrap it around the other screw terminal post.

36. Stack your larger magnets on top of one another so that they will freely rotate, colour all the sides but not the ends with a permanent marker.

37. Put one of the larger magnets on one side of the acetate plastic, and a nut on the other, so the larger magnet is sitting directly over the coil and its north and south poles are aligned with the smaller magnets north and south poles below.

Section 9 - Mounting The Laser to The L Bracket

For this section you will need:
- __
- __

1. Take the neon tubing and cut it it into 6 (six) pieces approximately 6mm each.  Tip: If you measure the first cut, you can then use that piece as a reference for the other 5 (five).

2. Stand each piece of neon tubing on its end and use a sharp knife to split one wall of the neon tubing down the center.

3. Take both laser mounting brackets and put the 6mm pieces of neon tubing onto the outside ring of each laser mounting bracket in the appropriate spots. Roll them around so they now hug the inside wall of the ring.

4. Put a small dab of glue onto each piece of neon tubing, gluing it to the laser mounting brackets.

5. Slide the two wires coming out of the laser through the hole in the center of the rear laser disc.

6. Apply glue around the backside of the laser.

7. Slide the rear laser disc up to the back of the laser to glue it in place. Ensure that the disc is centered on the back of the laser.

8. Slide the focus ring into one of the laser mounting brackets. Ensure that the neon tubes are gripping the focus ring of the laser. This will allow you to adjust the focus by spinning the body of the laser.

9. Slide the laser mounting bracket and laser onto the L Bracket. Then slide the other laser mounting bracket on until it pinches the laser between the two mounting brackets.

10. Glue the first laser mounting bracket while it is butted up against mirror Frame B.

11. Glue the second laser mounting bracket while applying pressure on it towards the laser by pinching the back bracket, and the front of the laser between your fingers.

Section 12 - Assembling The Exernal Housing

For this section you will need:
- Neon Tubing
- Utility Knife
- Glue
- Glue Stick
- The External Housing Plates

1. Take a long piece of neon tubing and cut 2 pieces 8 cm long

2. Split one wall of each neon tubing all the way down the center.

3. Put the neon tubing along the edges of the sliding door.

4. Put glue along the sides of the opening in the sliding door plate.

5. Position the sliding door so that the neon tubes makes contact with the glue you lied down on either side of the opening on the sliding door plate. Make sure you glue only where the sliding door is, this makes sure that the neon tubes are straight and properly spaced. When you do this the door should be in the closed position, covering the hole in the sliding door plate. Add extra glue along the sides of the neon tubing, gluing it to the sliding door plate. Make sure not to glue the Neon tubing to the sliding door.

6. Allow the glue to set completely, then slide the door so it is only covering 1cm of the hole in the sliding door plate, then glue the Neon tubing to the sliding door plate again.

7. Slide the sliding door plate to the end of the neon tubing, and finish gluing the neon tubing to the sliding door plate.

8. Position the bottom plate so that the sliding door is sitting to the left of the opening.

9. Take Side Plate 1 (The one with the L cut in it) and apply glue to the edge nearest the long side of the L.

10. Place the glued edge down on top of the bottom plate along it’s side. Use the Front plate as a square by butting it up against Side Plate 1 to ensure that it is sitting up at a 90 degree angle from the bottom plate. Let the glue set fully.

11. Take Side Plate 2 (The one without the L cut in it) and apply glue to the a long edge.

12. Place the glued edge down on top of the bottom plate along it’s side. Use the Front plate as a square by butting it up against Side Plate 1 to ensure that it is sitting up at a 90 degree angle from the bottom plate. Let the glue set fully.

13. Take Front Plate 1 and apply glue to the edges of one of the long sides and both of the short sides. Slide End Plate 1 in between the two side plates at one end of the bottom plate. All edges should be flush.

14. Repeat step 11 with End Plate 2. Your outer housing should look like this.

Section 13 - Mounting The Circuit

For this section you will need:
- Neon Tubing
- The Circuit

1. Take the Neon tubing and cut 4 (four) 15mm pieces.

2. Take the circuit and glue the pieces of neon tubing to the black circles, so that the hole in the tubing is positioned up and down. These will be used for mounting the circuit to the Side Plate A (The one with L)

3. Take the circuit and position it so the bolt holes line up with the neon tubing.

4. Screw the bolts through the bolt holes in Side Plate A (The one with the L) and into the neon tubing glued on the circuit.

Section 14 - Mounting The Internal Assembly Into The Outer Housing

For this section you will need:
- The Internal Assembly
- Glue
- Glue Stick
- Small Flathead Screwdriver

1. Take 3 extenders and 3 screws. Insert the screws into the 3 holes in the top outer housing plate and thread the extenders onto them until snug

2. Lay the plate down so that the extenders are facing up. Place the internal assembly on top of the extenders so that the 3 holes in the L-Frame line up with the extenders. Thread one screw into each hole until snug.

3.Cut 4 (four) 10mm sections of neon tube

4. Apply glue halfway around outer wall, over the entire length of the tube.

5. Place the tube in a corner of the outer housing, flush with the top edge of the walls

6. Repeat steps 2 and 3 for the remaining neon tubes. When finished you will have one tube in each corner.

7. Place down the top plate, completing the housing so the two arrows point at eachother, and so the four holes of the plate line up with the neon tubes that you glued on.

8. Insert the 4 screws into the holes of the top plate, screwing into the neon tubes.

9. Ensure that you can see both mirrors and the front of the laser through the sliding door on the bottom of the outer housing.

10. Refer to Scotts instructions for connecting the printer to the circuit

Section 15 - Assembling The Drip System

For this section you will need:
- __
- __

1. Take a dripper and cut off about 5cm from the fine tip end. We will now call the fine tip end the drip dispenser tube, and the large end the drip receiver tube.

2. Split the top of the drip receiver tube with your utility knife, then make a second cut at a 90 degree angle from the first, so you have cut an X into the top of the drip receiver tube with each cut being about 5mm deep.

3. Take the drip dispenser tube and cut the fine tip so it is about 3mm long.

4. Insert the fine tip of the drip dispenser tube into the top of the drip receiver about half way like shown.

5. Take a T-pin and puncture it through the sidewall of the drip receiver tube, so that it penetrates through both walls of the drip receiver tube, and the drip dispenser tube in the middle.

6. Push a second pin into the outside the drip receiver tube, about _mm from the tip of the end inside the drip receiver tube.

7. Cut two pieces of clear hose - one about 9” and the other about 3”. Slide the short piece over the top end of the drip counter, and the long piece over the bottom.

8. Take the valve and connect it to top end of the drip system. Cut another piece of clear hose and connect it to the other end of the valve. Make sure that the entire length of the drip system once connected is long enough to reach from your top reservoir to your bottom reservoir. We recommend making it about 18-20” long.

9. Take the audio jack and put the black wire onto the top T-pin, and the yellow wire onto the bottom T-pin. Tape the blue wire back, as we will not be needing this right now. Do not cut it off, as in the future this may be used to add additional functionality to the Peachy Printer.

10. Cut a piece of neon tubing about 3” long and glue it to the outer edge of the U-connector.

11. Cut a small piece of hose that reaches from the U-connector to the bottom of the top reservoir, and connect that piece to one end of the U-connector. Connect the top hose of the drip system to other side of the U-connector.

12. Take your entire drip system and hang the U connector over the edge of your top reservoir, so the short end hangs inside the reservoir, and the long end hangs down on the outside of the reservoir.

13. Take the bottom end of the hose and put it in the lower reservoir.

14. Use glue to take the hose in place at multiple points for support.

15. Your drip system should like look this.
