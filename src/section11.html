Section 11 - Assembling the Drip system

Description: In this section you'll be assembling the drip system. The drip system uses a siphoning action to drip saltwater from the top reservoir into the bottom reservoir raising the height of the resin. The microphone cables will be hooked up to the pieces of aluminum rod so it can detect when a drip occurs. By counting the number of drips the Peachy Printer is able to know what height the resin is currently at, so it knows what layer to print.

Note: We forgot to include your ABS valve pieces in the kit. They were priority mailed to you 2 days after we shipped your kit. In the meantime feel free to build your drip system, you can add the valve on when it arrives. We apologize for the inconvenience!

Recommendation: If using our standard setup, we recommend using a lower reservoir with a maximum height of 6 cm. If you can’t find anything of that size, cutting the bottom of an empty and clean milk or cream container to that height is an easy solution. While we realize that our recommendation provides you with a very small build volume, we strongly suggest that you use it as a starting point. Using a standard system make troubleshooting much easier. In addition, We've yet to test large prints. For that reason it's a good idea to start with what we know works, and right now that is prints less than 4 cm tall.

Note: You must choose reservoirs that have the same distance from wall to wall all the way up (walls that are not tapered). If the reservoir is larger at the top than it is at the bottom, larger at the bottom than it is at the top, or larger in the middle than it is at the top or bottom it will not work.

You also must make sure that your upper reservoir is no larger than your lower reservoir. In fact, it’s best if it is somewhat smaller to ensure that your lower reservoir will never overflow.

For this section you will need:
  • Clear Tubing - Note: If you need more clear tubing for your custom setup you can buy it from most pet stores (it's airline tubing for aquariums).
  • Neon Tubing - Note: If you need more neon tubing for your custom setup you can buy it from most hobby stores (it's Nitro RC fuel line).
  • Chopped syringe
  • 2 Long bolts
  • 2 Nuts
  • ABS valve pieces
  • Cardboard piece labelled "top"
  • Hose Hangers
  • Needle nose pliers with side cutter
  • Wire stripper
  • Utility knife
  • Ruler (metric)
  • Small file (must have sharp angle for precision filing)
  • Small sandpaper square
  • Hollow Aluminum Rod
  • Audio Cable
  • Upper and Lower liquid reservoirs

1. Take the Hollow Aluminum Rod and cut it in half using the utility knife (roll the rod back and forth while applying pressure with the knife). Make sure that the aluminum rod doesn’t get crushed when you cut it.
2. Take the rod with the closed end and file the points on both creased edges of the closed end. Once finished, the closed end should be no wider than the rest of the rod.
3. On the same piece of rod file nearly halfway through the aluminum rod creating a small hole positioned in the middle of the rod. Be careful not to bend the rod.
4. Disassemble the chopped syringe. Take the black gasket off of the plunger. Throw the plunger out, keep the gasket.
5. Cut two 1 cm pieces of neon tubing.
6. Take one of the pieces of neon tubing and push it over the small hole on the bottom of the syringe (the part where a needle would go). Set the syringe aside.
7. Take the other 1 cm piece of neon tubing and push it into the black plunger gasket.
8. Take the Rod with 2 open ends and push it through the Neon Tubing in the black plunger gasket until the rod protrudes through the gasket about 1 cm.

Note: We accidentally forgot to pre-poke a hole in some of the gaskets. If you’re gasket doesn’t have a pre-poked hole then use a pin, tack or utility knife to poke a hole in the center of the peak of the gasket.

9. Take the Audio Jack and cut off one end, then strip about 4 inches off the end. Cut off the insulating thread.
10. Sand off the protective coating on the ends of all three wires, being careful not to break the wires.
11. Twist the sanded ends of the red and blue wires together.
12. Cut one 6 cm piece of neon tube and one 2 cm piece of neon tube.
13. Take the red and blue wires and insert it into the 6 cm piece of neon tubing.
14. Take the aluminum rod with the plunger on it and slide halfway into the 6 cm piece of neon tubing on the end opposite the plunger. The wire should be pinched between the neon tube and the aluminum rod so that it can make an electrical connection.
15. Insert the Rod and Plunger into the large opening of the syringe.
16. Insert the sanded end of the remaining copper wire into the 2 cm piece of neon tube. Slide the open end of the aluminum rod that you filed a hole in halfway into the 2 cm piece of neon tubing on the same end that you inserted the wire. The wire should be pinched between the neon tube and the aluminum rod so that it can make an electrical connection. There should be approximately 1 cm of neon tubing extending off the end of the aluminum rod.
17. Push the closed end of the rod through the neon tubing at the bottom of the syringe until the hole you filed in the aluminum rod is inside the syringe. You may need to adjust the neon tubing on the bottom of the aluminum rod to allow it to travel far enough into the syringe. Water needs to flow through this hole from within the syringe so it must not be blocked.

Note: If the closed end of the rod doesn’t fit into the bottom of the syringe you might need to file the edges of the aluminum rod more to make it fit, but make sure it is still closed (water must not be able to get in the pinched end).

18. Cut one 6 cm piece of neon tubing.
19. Apply glue all the way around the outer edge of the Hose Hanger and bend the 6 cm piece of neon tubing around the outer edge of the hose hanger. Hold it until the glue sets. It’s important that the hose is glued well enough that it follows the curve of the Hose Hanger.
20. Gather your drinking straws, clear tubing, and upper and lower reservoirs. The clear tubing must be able to fit inside the drinking straws, if it doesn’t you’ll need larger straws. The straws will be glued to the upper and lower reservoirs to help route the hose on the drip feed.
21. Measure the depth of your upper reservoir and make note of it (you’ll need this measurement in another step later on). Cut a piece of a drinking straw to be 1 cm shorter than the measurement. Glue this piece of straw to the inside wall of your upper reservoir so that it’s flush with the top edge. There should be a 1 cm gap between the bottom of the straw and the bottom of the reservoir.
22. Repeat step 18 on the lower reservoir.
23. You must now cut sections of clear hose to make each end of your drip system reach into the upper and lower reservoirs. If you are sticking with our default printer setup then continue following the steps using our suggested measurements. If you are making your own custom reservoir setup, the length of these clear tubing sections will depend on the size of your upper and lower reservoirs, as well as the total distance between the two reservoirs. Follow the steps ahead but substitute the measurements needed for your custom setup in place of the suggested ones.
24. Take the clear tubing and cut it to a length of 30 cm. Slide the clear hose into the neon tubing on the bottom of the syringe.
25. Cut a piece of clear tubing to a length of 15 cm. Slide the clear hose into the neon tubing on top the aluminum rod coming out of the top of the syringe. Slide the other end into either side of the neon hose on the hose hanger.
26. Cut a piece of clear tubing to the same length you cut the straw for the upper reservoir. Slide the clear tubing into the neon tubing on the other side of the hose hanger.
27. Take the ABS Valve pieces and use the two long bolts & nuts to fasten the pieces together with the neon tubing on the top of your syringe pinched in between. The bolt in the round hole should be tightened and left alone, while the bolt in the slotted hole should be used for adjusting. This makes adjusting easier, and provides a lever action that’s more accurate.

Tip: To make adjusting easier you can apply a small dab of glue where the nut meets the plastic on the valve. This will prevent you from having to hold the nut while adjusting the valve - just be sure you don’t get any glue on the threads!
28. Put the cardboard piece labelled “top” on the cardboard stand, then place your upper reservoir on top.
29. Feed the short end of the clear tubing coming from your hose hanger into the straw on the inside of your upper reservoir and set the hose hanger on the rim of your upper reservoir so that the drip feed is hanging from it. Feed the clear tubing at the bottom of the drip feed into the straw inside the lower reservoir.

Congratulations, you've assembled the Peachy Printer!
