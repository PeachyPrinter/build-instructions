Section 5 - Adding the Spring Force Magnets and Coils to the Mirror Assembly

Description: In this section you’ll be assembling the spring force adjustment tubes, and mounting them along with the coils to the mirror assembly. The spring force adjustment tubes adjust the natural resting position (orientation) of the mirrors. The coils receive the signal coming from your computer’s audio jack and drive the movement of the mirrors, completing the Peachy’s galvonometers.
For this section you will need:

- Clear tubing
- Large square magnets
- Coils
- Both Mirror assemblies
- Utility knife
- Needle nose pliers
- spring force mounting plate
- permanent marker or pencil

Click to see an image of the parts labelled.

1. Insert the needle nosed pliers into the clear tubing while closed and then open them to stretch the opening of the tube.
2. Insert the square magnets into the stretched end of the clear tubing so that the north and south poles are sideways, touching the walls of the tubing. If you inserted the magnets in the correct orientation, they should attract to each other through the walls of the tubing.
3. Cut the clear tubing 3 cm (about 1") back from the stretched end.
4. Repeat steps 2 - 4 until you have four 3 cm tubes with magnets inside them. *If your magnets do not attract like this, they are not positioned correctly*. We will now call these the spring force adjustment tubes.
5. Clip the mirror frame into the Spring Force Mounting Plate as you did in Step 1 of Section 1.
6. Insert the non-magnet end of a spring force adjustment tube into the partial hole that extends off of the slot on the spring force mounting plate. Pull the tube through the hole until the magnet itself is right inside the hole. The excess tube must extend off of the mounting plate in the opposite direction of the mirror assembly.
7. Rotate the spring force adjustment tube until the face of the acetate arrow is level with the face of the mirror assembly frame. The dynamic damper arm must be extending away from the spring force adjustment tube.
8. Using a permanent marker, mark on the spring force adjustment tube the side that is closest to the thread assembly. Remove the spring force adjustment tube from the hole and glue the coil to the marked side centered directly on the magnet. Make sure that the coil is positioned so that the leads extend in the same direction as the excess tube coming off the magnet.
9. Insert the spring force adjustment tube and coil back into the hole on the spring force adjustment plate. It should look like the last picture in this step.
10. Insert another spring force adjustment tube into the second hole on the mounting plate. If necessary, rotate the adjustment tube so the face of the acetate arrow is parallel with the face of the mirror assembly frame.
11. Repeat steps 5-10 on the other mirror assembly.
