Section 13 - Building a Mount & Reservoir Stand

Description: In this section you’ll be building your mount and reservoir stand. This is what you’ll use to suspend your Peachy above the print area, and set your upper reservoir on. A stand can be made out of many things! In this section we’ll provide you with a paper template that you can download, print out, and use to make our standard sized stand out of cardboard. We’ll also provide you with some examples of how you can make a stand from scratch.

Note: We highly recommend that you do Part A and get your Peachy working using our standard setup first before building your own larger custom setup. It will be much easier to troubleshoot problems if you start with the standard setup.

Part A - Using the templates to make your stand.
For this section you will need:
  • Cardboard
  • White glue, wood glue, hot glue, or duct tape
  • Scissors
  • Utility knife
  • Computer with an internet connection
  • Printer (2D)The Peachy Printer
  • The Peachy Printer

Click to see an image of the parts labelled.

1. Go to http://instructions.peachyprinter.com/stand-template and download the file.
2. Print the template off on your 2D printer.
3. Cut the template pieces out of the paper and glue them to your cardboard.
4. Cut the cardboard in the shape of the templates you just glued on.
5. Glue the two side pieces to the back piece.
6. Allow the stand to dry. Once dry, stand it up on it's bottom edge. Mount the main hanger across the stand - the two nubs on the stand should slide into the slots on both ends of the main hanger to hold it in place. Set the piece labelled "top" aside for now, you'll need it in the next section.
Part B - Examples of other ways to build a stand
Example #1 - In this example we used a large cardboard box to mount the printer and upper reservoir. We simply poked holes in the top of the box and used two bolts and nuts to secure the printer to the inside of the top of the box.
Example #2 - In this example we used a milk crate to mount the printer and upper reservoir. We drilled four holes in the top of the crate and used zip ties to secure the printer to the milk crate. We also cut the front face out of the milk crate to allow easy access.
Example #3 - In this example we used a shelving unit to mount the printer and upper reservoir. Just like the milk crate, we drilled four holes in the shelf and used zip ties to secure the printer to it.
